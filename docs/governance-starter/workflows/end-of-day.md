# End of Day

> Before you close your laptop, take 5 minutes to leave things tidy.
> Tomorrow-you will appreciate it.

## 5-Minute Closeout

### 1. Save Your State (1 min)
- [ ] All changes committed? (Run `git status` — nothing should be hanging)
- [ ] If work is in progress, commit what you have with a `WIP:` prefix so you don't lose it

### 2. Update the Health Check (2 min)
Open `rules/HOW-ITS-BUILT.md` and ask yourself:
- Did I change how any part of the project works today?
- If yes, is the doc updated?

Quick mental check:
- [ ] Are all tests still passing?
- [ ] Did I introduce any known issues? (Write them down if so)
- [ ] Is anything broken that I'm leaving for tomorrow? (Write it down)

### 3. Leave a Breadcrumb (2 min)
Write a quick note for tomorrow-you. Put it in your commit message or a sticky note:

> "Left off at: [what you were doing]. Next step: [what to do next].
> Watch out for: [anything tricky you noticed]."

This saves 20+ minutes of "where was I?" tomorrow morning.

---

## Why This Matters

Projects don't fall apart in one day. They fall apart when small messes accumulate over weeks. Five minutes of cleanup per day prevents the "I don't even know where to start" feeling that kills motivation.
